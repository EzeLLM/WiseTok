Three Recipes: Savory, Baking, and Beverage


RECIPE 1: Miso-Braised Short Ribs with Root Vegetables

Serves: 4 | Prep Time: 20 minutes | Braise Time: 3-4 hours | Oven Temperature: 325°F (163°C)

Ingredients:
- 3 lbs beef short ribs, trimmed of excess fat
- 3 tbsp white miso paste
- 4 tbsp mirin
- 2 tbsp sake (or dry sherry)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 inches fresh ginger root, peeled and sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup dashi or chicken stock
- 2 medium carrots, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 lb parsnips, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 small daikon radish (about 8 oz), cut into 1.5-inch cubes
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 scallions, thinly sliced on bias (for garnish)
- 1 tsp sesame seeds (for garnish)
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Blot short ribs dry with paper towels and season generously on all sides with salt and pepper.

2. Heat vegetable oil in a heavy Dutch oven or braising pan over high heat until shimmering. Working in batches to avoid crowding, sear short ribs until deep brown on all sides (4-5 minutes per side). Transfer to a plate.

3. Whisk together miso paste, mirin, sake, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and dashi in a small bowl until miso is dissolved. Add ginger and garlic to the braising liquid.

4. Return short ribs to the pot and pour the miso mixture over them. Liquid should come halfway up the sides of the meat; add water if needed. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.

5. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and transfer to preheated oven. Braise for 2 hours, turning ribs halfway through cooking.

6. Remove from oven and add carrots, parsnips, and daikon. Return to oven, covered, for 45-60 minutes until vegetables are tender and ribs are fork-tender.

7. Carefully remove ribs and vegetables to a serving platter. Skim fat from cooking liquid, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a saucepan. Reduce over high heat until slightly thickened and richly flavored, about 8-10 minutes. Pour sauce over ribs and vegetables.

8. Garnish with scallions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately with steamed jasmine rice or noodles.


RECIPE 2: Sourdough Focaccia with Rosemary and Sea Salt

Yield: One 10x14 inch focaccia | Active Time: 45 minutes | Passive Time: 18-24 hours

Ingredients:
- 500g bread flour (3.5 cups)
- 325g water (1 1/3 cups, 65% hydration)
- 100g mature sourdough starter (fed 4-6 hours prior, at peak rise)
- 10g fine sea salt (2 tsp)
- 6g instant yeast (1.5 tsp) [optional, aids rise]
- 30g extra virgin olive oil
- 6g coarse sea salt (1 tsp) for topping
- 3g fresh rosemary leaves, roughly chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Fleur de sel for finishing

Instructions:
1. Mix flour, water, sourdough starter, fine sea salt, and yeast (if using) in a large bowl. Let rest (autolyse) for 30 minutes uncovered.

2. Stretch and fold the dough every 30 minutes for 2 hours, performing 4 sets of turns total. Between turns, let the dough rest.

3. After final fold, shape into a rough ball and place in a lightly oiled container. Proof at room temperature (68-72°F / 20-22°C) for 12-18 hours until nearly doubled.

4. Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). Line a 10x14 inch baking pan with parchment paper.

5. Gently transfer dough to prepared pan. Using oiled fingers, dimple and stretch the dough to fill the pan, creating the characteristic pocked surface of focaccia. If dough resists, rest for 10 minutes and try again.

6. Drizzle 20g olive oil over the surface. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt, rosemary, and minced garlic. Let rest for 20 minutes.

7. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown and crisp. Brush with remaining 10g olive oil and sprinkle with fleur de sel immediately upon removal from oven.

8. Cool on a wire rack for at least 15 minutes before cutting and serving.


RECIPE 3: Aperol Spritz Variation with Citrus and Herbs

Yields: 2 cocktails | Prep Time: 5 minutes

Ingredients (per drink):
- 2 oz Aperol
- 3 oz Prosecco (Italian sparkling wine)
- 1.5 oz soda water (or to taste)
- 1/2 oz fresh lemon juice (about 1/2 medium lemon)
- 0.25 oz simple syrup (optional, adjust to taste)
- 2 fresh basil leaves
- 1 thin slice blood orange or regular orange
- Ice (large cubes or crushed ice, 1 cup per drink)
- Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:
1. Fill a wine glass or aperitivo glass with ice. Add basil leaves and gently muddle just until fragrant (do not crush).

2. Pour Aperol into the glass. Add fresh lemon juice and simple syrup (if using).

3. Top with Prosecco and soda water in a 3:1 ratio, or adjust to preference. Stir gently.

4. Express the orange slice over the drink by gently squeezing to release oils, then drop into glass as garnish.

5. Add a pinch of sea salt to the rim or stir into the drink.

6. Serve immediately. The cocktail should be light, refreshing, and not overly sweet—the Aperol's bittersweet herbal notes balanced by the citrus acidity.

Note: The Aperol Spritz is traditionally served as an aperitif in northeastern Italy during early evening hours. Prosecco and soda water proportions can be adjusted based on desired carbonation and sweetness levels.
