Campaign Speech: "The Future We Build Together"

October 14th, 2024 — Delivered at the Memorial Coliseum, Portland, Oregon


Good evening, everyone. Thank you for being here.

[APPLAUSE]

You know, I've traveled to forty-seven states in the past eighteen months. I've sat in living rooms and diners and town halls from Maine to California. And everywhere I go, I hear the same thing. People are tired. They're tired of being told that politics is something that happens to them, something orchestrated by distant figures in marble buildings. They're tired of being treated as an afterthought.

But here's what I also hear: hope. Real, stubborn, American hope. And that hope is grounded in something real. It's grounded in the understanding that we—all of us, together—have the power to build a future that actually works.

We will restore integrity to our institutions.

We will restore it.

[APPLAUSE]

We will invest in schools that don't ask families to choose between buying textbooks and buying insulin. We will invest in them, and we will ensure that a child's zip code is not a sentence, not a prediction of their future potential.

We will build an economy that works for working people, not just for the wealthy few who have learned to game the system. We will do this. We will do it by holding corporations accountable. We will do it by ensuring that wages keep pace with the cost of living.

And let me tell you something: this is not radical. This is not extreme. This is common sense.

[APPLAUSE, CHEERING]

Now, I know there are those who will say we cannot afford it. They will say we cannot invest in infrastructure, in education, in healthcare without bankrupting ourselves. But I ask you this: What is the cost of doing nothing? What is the cost when families go bankrupt over a single medical emergency? What is the cost when our infrastructure crumbles? What is the cost when we lose an entire generation of students because we failed to fund the schools they depend on?

The real luxury—the real indulgence—is inaction.

My opponent says that we should trust the free market to solve these problems. But the free market has had fifty years. And what has it delivered? Stagnant wages. Environmental catastrophe. Healthcare outcomes worse than every other developed nation on Earth. Is that the vision you want for your children?

I don't think so.

[APPLAUSE, CHEERING]

We have tried trickle-down economics. We have tried deregulation. And the only thing that trickled down was the tax burden onto the shoulders of working families while billionaires got richer. That ends on my watch.

We will raise the minimum wage to a living wage. Do you know what that means? It means a single parent working full-time will not have to choose between rent and food. It means dignity. It means respect.

We will protect Social Security and Medicare. We will protect them because our seniors have earned the right to retire with dignity. And yet my opponent would privatize these programs. He would turn your grandmother's security into a bet in the stock market. I will not allow it.

[APPLAUSE]

On climate change, we will lead. We will transition to clean energy not because it is noble—though it is—but because it is economically smart. We will create millions of good-paying jobs in wind and solar and energy efficiency. We will do it right here, in America, instead of handing that future to China.

Some say climate action is too expensive. I say inaction is too expensive. What is the cost of the wildfires we've seen? What is the cost of the hurricanes getting stronger? What is the cost of farmland becoming uninhabitable?

We will act.

[APPLAUSE]

And on democracy itself, we will protect it with everything we have. Democracy is not guaranteed. It is fragile. It requires constant vigilance. My opponent has made it clear that he does not believe in the peaceful transfer of power. He has made it clear that he believes he is above the law. I believe in democracy. I believe in the Constitution. And I will defend both.

[STANDING OVATION]

So here is what I'm asking you: Don't just show up in November. Don't just vote. Bring someone with you. Make a phone call. Have a conversation with a neighbor who might disagree with you. Do not retreat into your corner. Meet in the middle. Meet in living rooms. Meet in diners. Because that is how change happens. Change happens when ordinary people decide that the future is worth fighting for.

The future we build will be built by teachers and nurses and electricians and farm workers. It will be built by people who believe that their neighbor's success is their success. It will be built by people who understand that we are all in this together.

I believe in the American people. I believe in your capacity for greatness. I believe that the best days of this nation are not behind us—they are ahead of us. But only if we have the courage to claim them.

Thank you, and may God bless us all.

[STANDING OVATION, EXTENDED APPLAUSE]
